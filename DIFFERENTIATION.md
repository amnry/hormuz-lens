# Differentiation

Capability matrix against the four public Strait of Hormuz trackers we benchmarked. Read as "what does this project do that the incumbents don't," not as competitive trash talk; several of these sites do their narrower jobs well.

| Capability                            | hormuz.lens | HormuzTracker.com | hormuzmonitor.com | hormuztracking.com | marinevesseltraffic.com |
| ------------------------------------- | :---------: | :---------------: | :---------------: | :----------------: | :---------------------: |
| Live vessel map                       | ✅          | ❌ (aggregates only) | ✅              | ✅                 | ✅                      |
| 3D globe view                         | ✅          | ❌                | ❌                | ❌                 | ❌                      |
| Historical replay from Jan 1          | ✅          | ❌ (daily snapshots) | ❌             | Partial            | ❌                      |
| Grounded chatbot over the data        | ✅          | ❌                | ❌                | ❌                 | ❌                      |
| Open source                           | ✅          | ❌                | ❌                | ❌                 | ❌                      |
| Statistical correlation (transits × Brent, flag-mix shifts) | ✅ | Partial | ❌ | ❌ | ❌ |
| Transponder honesty disclosure (spoofing, dark vessels, AIS gaps) | ✅ | ❌ | ❌ | ❌ | ❌ |

The unique intersection is the last four rows: an open-source, replayable dataset with a grounded analyst on top and an explicit note on AIS blind spots. Incumbents stop at a live map or at static daily aggregates; none expose the underlying data or acknowledge transponder gaps.
